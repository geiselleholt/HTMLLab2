Guam's Rich Culture

Guam's culture is a unique blend of Chamorro, Spanish, and American influences. The Chamorro people have a rich tradition of dance, music, and storytelling, which are passed down from generation to generation. Some of the most popular Chamorro traditions include the Inarajan Fiestan Tasi, a celebration of the sea, and the Festival of Pacific Arts, a gathering of artists and performers from around the Pacific region. Guam is also known for its delicious cuisine, which features a mix of Chamorro, Filipino, Japanese, and American dishes. Some of the most popular foods in Guam include red rice, kelaguen, and shrimp patties. In addition to its cultural attractions, Guam is also home to a number of natural wonders, including Tumon Bay, Two Lovers Point, and Ritidian Point.
