History of Guhan

Guam has a rich history that dates back thousands of years. The indigenous people of Guam are known as the Chamorros, who are believed to have migrated to the island from Southeast Asia around 2000 B.C. The Chamorros lived in small villages and practiced fishing, farming, and other traditional activities. In the 16th century, Guam was colonized by Spain and remained under Spanish rule until 1898, when it was ceded to the United States after the Spanish-American War. Guam was occupied by Japan during World War II and was later recaptured by the United States. In 1950, Guam became an unincorporated territory of the United States, and its residents were granted U.S. citizenship in 1952. Today, Guam is a popular tourist destination and a strategic military outpost for the U.S. military.
